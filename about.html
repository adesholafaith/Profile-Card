About Me

Bio

I'm Adeshola Faith and I am currently serving in the NYSC program. I build projects using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and I enjoy bringing concepts to life through beautiful, user-friendly interfaces.

I believe great design is about clarity and experience, not just colors and shapes. Right now, I'm learning Git and GitHub, exploring new frontend frameworks, and continuously sharpening my JavaScript skills.

Background

I graduated from the University of Ilorin where I studied microbiology. Before I graduated, my love for design and creativity pushed me to transition into tech. Since then, I have been continuously learning and practicing web development. I completed a frontend development course and then applied for internship at HNG, which I was accepted into.

Goals in this program

By the end of the program, I hope to:

  • Become comfortable with modern CSS and JavaScript frameworks.
  • Practice responsive layouts and improved UX patterns.
  • Gain opportunities that help me further improve my skills.

Areas of low confidence

I'm still building confidence in advanced JavaScript concepts such as asynchronous programming, APIs, and state management. I also find frontend frameworks like React and Angular a bit challenging at the moment, especially when it comes to understanding their component structures and data flow. In addition, I'm working on improving my version control skills with Git and GitHub, as well as learning how to collaborate more effectively on larger projects.

Extra thoughts

This journey has taught me that learning frontend development is as much about patience and practice as it is about creativity. There are days when things don't work, and that's okay. Every bug fixed or layout improved is a step forward.
I've realized that consistency matters more than speed. Even small efforts each day build into something meaningful over time. I want to keep exploring new tools, stay curious about design and user experience, and never lose the excitement that got me started in the first place.
Most importantly, I want to remember that progress isn't always visible immediately, but it's happening quietly with every project, every challenge, and every lesson learned.
